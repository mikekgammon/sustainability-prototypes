priority_highThe cross-scope case to watch

The EV van project is the kind of intervention the current platform can't model honestly. Diesel goes down (Scope 1 saving) but electricity goes up (Scope 2 cost). The platform needs to track both legs separately and show the net carbon impact, otherwise customers either over-claim savings (count only the diesel reduction) or under-deliver (don't notice the electricity bill going up). Same pattern applies to heat pumps replacing gas boilers, and any electrification project.

What the chart tells Northford

Scope 1 is the biggest absolute reduction (−1,128 tCO₂e), driven by the HVO switch. Scope 2 barely moves because the EV project adds electricity load that nearly cancels the LED savings. Scope 3 reduction is real but smaller — supplier-led work has slower carbon delivery per £ spent.

What the platform has to handle

Three different data shapes feeding one chart: Scope 1 from non-kWh activity data (litres, kg), Scope 2 from kWh with grid intensity over time, Scope 3 from spend-based and supplier-reported totals. The EV project alone touches two scopes simultaneously. That's the modelling architecture Scope 3 Project Modelling unlocks.
