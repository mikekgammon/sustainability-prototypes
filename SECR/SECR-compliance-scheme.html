1

What is SECR?

Streamlined Energy and Carbon Reporting (SECR) is a UK Government regulation requiring qualifying organisations to report their energy consumption and associated greenhouse gas emissions in their annual directors' report. It replaced the CRC Energy Efficiency Scheme in April 2019.

True's sustainability team currently produces SECR reports manually using Excel templates. The goal is to automate this within TrueDI, creating a reusable pattern that future compliance schemes can follow.

What's Reported

Energy Consumption

Total kWh across stationary combustion, mobile combustion, electricity, generated electricity, and business travel — with year-on-year comparison and intensity metric.

What's Reported

Carbon Emissions

Total tCO2e calculated two ways: location-based (grid average factors) and market-based (accounting for renewable energy certificates). Broken down by the same categories.

What's Reported

Intensity Metric

Energy and carbon normalised by a chosen business metric — e.g. kWh/pupil, tCO2e/£m turnover, kWh/employee. Enables meaningful year-on-year comparison.

lightbulb

Pattern setter. SECR is the first compliance scheme to be built. The data structures, UI patterns, and calculation engine designed here will be reused for CCA, ESOS, RE100, and future schemes. Getting this right is critical.

Platform Mapping — What TrueDI Already Has

Most of the data required for SECR already exists within the TrueDI platform. The key question is what can be auto-populated versus what needs manual entry.

Auto-populated

Site Information

Site names, addresses, MPAN/MPRN references are already held in the platform from meter onboarding.

Auto-populated

Electricity Consumption

HH and nHH meter data already flows into the platform. Can be summed per site for the reporting period.

Auto-populated

Gas Consumption

Gas meter data (where available) already in platform. Can be mapped to sites and reporting periods.

Manual Entry

Fleet & Transport Data

Diesel, petrol, and vehicle data from fuel cards or mileage logs. Not currently in the platform — needs a manual input interface.

Manual Entry

Business Travel

Mileage claims, unknown car type. Typically from expense systems. Needs manual entry or CSV upload.

Manual Entry

Intensity Metric

Client-specific: turnover, headcount, GIA, pupils. Needs to be entered once per reporting period per client.

Manual Entry

REC Coverage

Renewable Energy Certificate % per electricity meter. Determines market-based emissions calculation.

System Managed

Conversion Factors

UK Government GHG factors updated annually. Should be stored centrally and auto-applied based on reporting year. Blended weighting needs to be automatic.

Conversion Factor Engine

The conversion factor system is the calculation backbone of SECR. It handles two separate lookups — energy conversion (raw units → kWh) and carbon conversion (kWh → tCO2e) — each with year-blended weighting.

How Blended Weighting Works

SECR reporting periods often span calendar years (e.g. Sep 2023 – Aug 2024). Since UK Government conversion factors are published per calendar year, the template blends them proportionally based on how many days fall in each year.

Example — KES Bath Previous Period

Sep 2023 – Aug 2024

2023 weighting: 0.333 (4 months: Sep–Dec)
2024 weighting: 0.667 (8 months: Jan–Aug)
2025 weighting: 0.000

Example — KES Bath Current Period

Sep 2024 – Aug 2025

2023 weighting: 0.000
2024 weighting: 0.334 (4 months: Sep–Dec)
2025 weighting: 0.666 (8 months: Jan–Aug)

Key Design Decisions

Data Gaps

Flag, Don't Block

When monthly consumption data has gaps, show a warning with the gap period and offer options: extrapolate from adjacent months (like Sohil does manually), enter a manual override, or mark as estimated. Every estimated value should carry an audit flag.

Conversion Factors

Invisible Unless Wrong

Factors should be applied automatically based on the reporting period dates. The blended weighting should calculate itself. Only surface the factors if there's an error or the user explicitly wants to inspect them.

Fleet & Travel

Simple Entry Table

A lightweight input table for resources not in the platform: resource type, value, unit. Support CSV upload for fuel card data. Keep it visually distinct from auto-populated data.

Output

Match the Regulation Format

The disclosure table format is well-defined (see Section 4). Don't redesign it — replicate it faithfully so clients can copy-paste directly into their annual report. Add a methodology statement auto-generated from the data.

Audit Trail

Every Number Has a Source

Platform data: link back to meters + date range. Manual entries: show who entered, when. Estimates: show method used. Conversion factors: show which year(s) and weighting. This is critical for compliance credibility.

Reusability

Abstract the Pattern

Build the "reporting period setup + site selection + data review + output" flow as a generic compliance framework. SECR-specific logic (categories, factors, output format) should be configuration, not hard-coded.

architecture

Otto's principle applies here: "Numbers come in, crunch a couple, numbers come out." The SECR module should be a thin layer on top of a generic calculation engine. The resource → conversion factor → output pipeline is the same for every compliance scheme — only the specific factors and output format change.

1 How do we handle Solar PV where no meter data exists? KES Bath used estimated annual generation from installation certificates. Should we support a "certified estimate" input type? Sohil / Otto Open
2 Should conversion factors be updatable by the sustainability team, or locked to a central system-managed table? Current Excel lets Sohil override factors — is that needed in platform? Otto Open
