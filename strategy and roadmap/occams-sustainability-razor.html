Occam's razor — the sustainability platform cut

A 17-page roadmap meeting compressed to three jobs and three priorities.
personMike Gammon · Lead Product Designer groupsSource: Otto · Paul roadmap meeting calendar_todayMay 2026

The meeting reads as twenty-plus features but it's really one product doing one thing, three ways.

Strip it back and the platform answers a single question for the customer: what should I do next, and is it working? Underneath that question sit three irreducible jobs — Ingest, Decide, Prove. Every other feature discussed (virtual contracts, Monte Carlo, AI optimisation, soft credit checks, funding integration) is a refinement of one of those three, not a fourth job.

This document maps the meeting onto the cut, separates v1 essentials from later sophistication, and lands the three strategic priorities that follow.

The principle

"Entities should not be multiplied beyond necessity."

Translated to product: if a feature doesn't make ingestion cleaner, decisions sharper, or proof easier — it's premature.

William of Ockham · 14th century

The test

If a feature doesn't make ingestion cleaner, decisions sharper, or proof easier — it's premature. Park it until the foundation is solid.

3

Three implications

What the cut tells us to do

Three insights fall out of the analysis — one about the bottleneck, one about retention, one about commercial entry. They define the order of work.

01
Ingestion is the bottleneck under everything else
Until data flows in cleanly, every layer above is constrained. No amount of clever scenario modelling fixes the underlying complaint that customers are entering proposals manually, reviewing invoices by hand, and waiting on spreadsheets. Fix the pipe before designing what flows through it.
Priority — fix first
02
Strategy V2 is where Decide closes the loop
It's the only screen in the platform where forecast meets live actuals meets the prompt to act. That's why Otto's flagged it as retention — it's the one place a customer comes back to weekly. Already in flight. Ship it next.
Priority — ship next
03
SECR is the cleanest commercial wedge
It's pure Prove, customers can't avoid it, they don't do it well, and TRUE already holds most of the data. Anywhere ingestion plus automation collapses a manual job is high-leverage — SECR is the most obvious one in the meeting. Strong candidate for the first dedicated workstream after Strategy V2.
Priority — wedge to market

The honest compression

Fix ingestion. Ship Strategy V2. Automate SECR. The rest follows once those three are in place.
